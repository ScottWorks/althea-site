End users load up their device or wifi router with blockchain tokens to pay for internet access.
Neighborhood transmitters forward packets and receive payment, competing to provide service.
Regional uplinks act as gateways to the internet and receive payment, competing with other uplinks.

The Internet currently relies on large, monopolistic ISPs. Due to the lack of meaningful competition, they are free to charge exorbitant rates, throttle speeds, and censor content.

We're creating a new Internet, a mesh network where routers are owned independently and compensated automatically for providing bandwidth to the network.

Althea is a set of internet protocols with payment built in, creating incentives for rapid expansion and investment in the network. At the same time, the ability for routers to be funded and compensated autonomously allows for a completely decentralized architecture without the need for governments or businesses.
